Instagram started in 2010 and focused on photos. Users could share pictures with filters to make them look cool. It was a hit right away. Visuals took over.


Over time, Instagram added Stories and Reels. Stories disappear after 24 hours, while Reels are short videos. It kept growing and stayed popular. Instagram turned sharing into an art form. It’s bright, colorful, and here to stay.


Instagram made social media all about images. When it launched, smartphones were getting better cameras, so it was perfect timing. People posted food, travel, and selfies with fun filters. By 2012, Facebook bought it for $1 billion because it was so big. Today, it has over a billion users. Features like Stories, added in 2016, let people share quick moments. Reels came later to compete with TikTok’s videos. Influencers—people who get famous online—use it to reach fans. Businesses sell products there too.


Instagram is different because it’s less about words and more about looks. That focus on beauty changed social media. Everyone became a photographer. From simple photos to videos, Instagram evolved fast. It showed the power of visuals. Next up: short videos take over!
