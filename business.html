Social media isn’t just for fun—it’s for business too. Companies use it to sell products and talk to customers. It started small but grew huge.


Platforms like Facebook and Instagram have ads and shops. Businesses can pay to reach more people. It’s a cheap way to market.Businesses jumped on social media as it got popular. They saw it as a way to connect with customers.


In the early days, they just made pages to chat with fans. Now, it’s a whole strategy. Facebook ads target people based on what they like—super smart! Instagram lets you buy stuff right from a post. TikTok has influencers who show off products in videos. By 2025, companies spend billions on social media ads. Small shops and big brands both use it. It’s not just selling; it’s building trust with customers. They can ask questions or complain online too. Social media turned into a giant store and customer service desk. It’s a big part of its evolution.


Even job hunting changed. People use LinkedIn to find work. Social media connects employers and workers. Businesses keep finding new ways to use these platforms. It’s a money-making machine now.
