Social media isn’t perfect. It has problems like fake news and bullying. As it grew, so did the issues. People started noticing. They saw how social media could be used to spread lies or hurt others.


Privacy is a big worry too. Platforms collect data about users. Sometimes, that info gets used in ways people don’t like. It’s a trade-off: you get to use social media for free, but you give up some privacy. This has led to debates about how much control people should have over their data.


The challenges of social media are serious. Fake news spreads fast, like during elections, confusing people. Cyberbullying hurts kids and adults, with mean comments or threats. Privacy got tricky when companies like Facebook sold data—there was a big scandal in 2018 about it. Users share so much, but they don’t always know who sees it. Then there’s addiction; some spend hours scrolling and feel bad after. Mental health can suffer from comparing lives online.


Fixing these issues isn’t easy. Platforms try with rules and filters, but it’s slow. Users have to be careful too. Challenges show social media’s power—and its risks. It’s not all fun. The last page looks ahead.
