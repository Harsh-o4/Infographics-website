In 2004, Facebook started at Harvard University. It was just for students at first, but it grew fast. By 2006, anyone could join. It became the king of social media.


Facebook was simple and clean, unlike MySpace. It focused on connecting people with real friends. Users could post updates, photos, and more. It was easy to use. Facebook changed the game for social media forever. You could share your life, join groups, and even play games. Businesses started using it too, to sell things or talk to customers.


Mark Zuckerberg created it as a way for college students to connect. Soon, it spread to other schools, then the whole world. By 2012, it had over a billion users—huge numbers! People liked it because it was less messy than MySpace and more personal. Today, it’s still one of the biggest platforms out there. It added features like video and Marketplace over time. Facebook made social media a daily habit for millions. It’s hard to imagine life without it now.


Other platforms tried to compete, but Facebook stayed strong. It bought Instagram and WhatsApp to grow even more. It’s a giant in the online world.


Facebook’s success showed that social media could be simple and powerful. It set the stage for new apps. The evolution kept going!
