The 2000s brought bigger social media platforms. Friendster launched in 2002 and let people connect with friends of friends. It grew fast and had millions of users. This was a new era.


Then came MySpace in 2003. It was colorful and let users customize their pages. People could add music, photos, and more. MySpace became a huge hit, especially with teens.ySpace changed social media in a big way. Before it, platforms were simple and plain. MySpace let users show their personality with bright backgrounds and songs. By 2006, it was the most visited site in the U.S., beating even Google. People used it to make friends, share art, and even find bands.


Friendster was popular too, but it had technical problems and faded. MySpace felt like a party online—everyone wanted to join. At its peak, it had over 100 million users. But it didn’t last forever; new sites were coming. Still, it showed how fun social media could be. The 2000s were a wild time online.


These platforms made social media about more than just talking. They were about creativity and showing who you are. Users loved having control. It was a big step forward.


Friendster and MySpace didn’t stay on top, but they mattered. They got millions of people hooked on social media. The next platforms would build on this success.
